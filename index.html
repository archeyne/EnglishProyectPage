Un Campus Verde: Integración de la Enseñanza de Inglés y Gestión de Residuos en la Universidad Nacional de Colombia

Welcome to the academic framework that bridges environmental conservation and higher-level technical learning. We utilize hands-on campus sorting and specialized greenhouse activities to foster active vocabulary building in Technical English while deploying a solid waste management workflow inside our university.

Project Objectives

Objetivos del Proyecto

Goal 01 / Meta 01

Audiovisual Documentary Product

Create an audiovisual documentary detailing campus recycling realities. This product records multiple interviews with students and experts around the campus to highlight recycling behaviors, system bottlenecks, and green incentives.

SMART Metrics / Métricas SMART

Specific: A documentary detailing student and expert views on campus waste.
Measurable: Compilation of 10+ expert and student video testimonies.
Attainable: Conduct campus interviews targeting diverse faculties.
Goal 02 / Meta 02

Technical Greenhouse English

Deliver technical English instruction contextually inside agricultural greenhouses. Students learn organic waste composting terminology, plant science, and industrial tools using practical English tasks.

SMART Metrics / Métricas SMART

Specific: Acquisition of 50 technical agricultural terms.
Measurable: 70% of participants pass the technical vocabulary audit.
Time-bound: Verified by week-8 post-intervention diagnostic.

Implementation Process

Proceso de Implementación

Phase 1: Environmental & Academic Diagnosis

Weeks 1 - 2

Initial deployment focusing on measuring waste behaviors and baseline communication competence. We execute thorough campus audits alongside student English capability diagnostics to model our vocabulary priorities.

Key Tasks / Tareas Clave

  • Audit sorting centers on campus
  • Administer English entry tests to 150 students
  • Catalog primary non-organic contamination culprits

Bilingual Glossary / Glosario

  • Waste audit: Auditoría de residuos
  • Contamination rate: Tasa de contaminación
  • Sorting baseline: Línea base de separación

Phase 2: Tactical Action & Infrastructure

Weeks 3 - 5

Active implementation of bilingual guidelines and technical infrastructure. We show to the students the two resource PDFs inside the greenhouses, but we did not install any sorting labels.

Key Tasks / Tareas Clave

  • Show students the two resource PDFs inside the greenhouses
  • Review technical vocabulary terms interactively
  • Distribute the Waste Management Kit to participants

Bilingual Glossary / Glosario

  • Organic compost: Abono orgánico
  • Greenhouse beds: Camas de invernadero
  • Bilingual signage: Señalización bilingüe
If the student wants to see more, they should open the AgroEnglish Resource Guide. / Si el estudiante desea ver más, debe abrir la Guía de Recursos AgroEnglish.

Phase 3: Interactive Sorting & Roleplay

Weeks 6 - 8

Active learning tasks! Students participate in interactive trash-sorting competitions utilizing the bilingual guides and engage in greenhouse role-play scenarios simulating agricultural export inspections.

Key Tasks / Tareas Clave

  • Organize the Bilingual Recycling Challenge
  • Conduct agricultural role-play sessions
  • Run exit exams & survey audit evaluations

Bilingual Glossary / Glosario

  • Role-play simulation: Simulación de rol
  • Disposable sorting: Clasificación de desechos
  • Proficiency exit: Salida de competencia

Key Finding 1 / Hallazgo 1

High Intent, Low System Trust

An overwhelming 78% of respondents believe recycling is important, yet only 11% view the university's recycling system as effective. The student body is bottlenecked by system quality.

78% importance vs. 11% effectiveness.
Key Finding 2 / Hallazgo 2

High Compliance Level

Despite low trust in infrastructure, individual willingness remains high: 64.2% of respondents separate waste "always" or "often", and 48.6% claim they recycle regularly.

64.2% separate waste regularly.
Key Finding 3 / Hallazgo 3

The "Coffee Cup" Trap

Overall sorting literacy is excellent (above 70% accuracy for standard items), but plastic-lined coffee cups are a critical blindspot, causing "wishful recycling" contamination in 39.4% of cases.

Only 45.9% correct sorting on coffee cups.

Strategic Action Plan / Plan de Acción

1
Overhaul Bin Signage

Over 33.9% requested better signage. New labels must explicitly target confusion points—clearly stating "No plastic-lined coffee cups" on white bins.

2
Launch Incentive Programs

Matched with the other 33.9% motivator, introducing gamified rewards (such as points redeemable at the campus bookstore) can drive consistent sorting behavior.

3
Increase System Transparency

By publishing monthly data on diverted waste volumes, the university can convert neutral or cynical students into active participants.

Challenges & Solutions / Desafíos y Soluciones

Implementing interactive programs on a busy public campus requires adapting to logistical challenges.

Challenge / Desafío

Low Initial Participation

Students are pressed for time, resulting in low attendance at optional sorting events.

Solution / Solución

Gamification & Course Integration

Coordinated with agriscience professors to award extra credit points and implemented friendly trash sorting speed drills.

Challenge / Desafío

The Waste Knowledge Gap

High willingness to recycle is undermined by confusion over campus-specific waste (e.g., local coffee cups, food containers). This leads to accidental contamination, which ruins otherwise recyclable batches.

Solution / Solución

Visual, Campus-Specific Icons

Replace generic labels with visual, campus-specific icons that show the exact items sold at university cafeterias. Pair this with a quick digital sorting guide during student and staff orientation.

Institutional Expansion Proposal / Propuesta

Scaling Sustainable Growth across UNAL Campuses

We propose establishing this integrated bilingual waste-management pedagogy as a standard curricular module. Campus authorities are invited to partner with us, download our complete deployment report, or request guides for other campus branches (Medellín, Palmira, Manizales).
